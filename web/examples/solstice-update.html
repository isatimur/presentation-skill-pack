Key investment

Cavendish Materials: the convergence thesis playing out.

Cavendish develops next-generation battery electrode materials using AI-assisted molecular design. When Solstice led the Series B in 2022, the thesis rested on three assertions: grid storage demand would accelerate faster than incumbent supply, a small materials advantage compounds non-linearly over time, and the team had done this before.

All three have proven correct. Q2 brought a long-term offtake agreement with a major European grid operator — the first institutional validation of the materials platform at commercial scale.

The offtake represents $340M in contracted revenue over seven years — before a single new product is introduced.

Market outlook

Tailwinds

Grid infrastructure supercycle. $4.7T in committed global grid investment through 2035. Cavendish and Fenwick Climate are directly exposed.

AI infrastructure demand. Meridian Compute's colocation thesis continues to strengthen as hyperscaler capex accelerates at 40%+ annually.

Healthcare consolidation. Prism Health's target acquirers are under pressure to show AI-driven differentiation before 2027 refresh cycles.

Market outlook

Headwinds

Rate environment. Elevated rates compress exit multiples on later-stage software assets. Cobalt Systems review reflects this directly.

IPO window remains narrow. Vantage Logistics and Meridian Compute's 2027 IPO targets assume window reopens. Monitoring closely.

LP liquidity pressure. Certain LPs face DPI pressure. We have pre-agreed secondary capacity for up to $80M at structured terms.

ESG highlight

Climate impact becoming a competitive advantage, not a constraint.

Solstice's 2023 commitment to Paris-aligned portfolio construction has, counterintuitively, expanded rather than constrained our opportunity set. The two fastest-growing positions in the portfolio — Cavendish and Fenwick — are also the two most climate-forward.

In Q2 we completed our first portfolio-wide scope 3 emissions mapping. Results were materially better than industry benchmarks. We believe this will become a significant LP selection criterion by 2027, and Solstice is positioned ahead of that shift.
