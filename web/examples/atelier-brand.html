IX
Founding Story  ·  2019
A workshop in Lyon.
One promise.
Seven years of consequence.

Atelier No. 9 was born in a rented workshop in Lyon's Croix-Rousse quarter, where silk-weavers once worked before the machines arrived. Founders Margot Sérier and Cédric Villette had each spent a decade in luxury goods manufacturing — she in bag construction for a Parisian maison, he in full-grain sourcing across the Córdoba tanneries — when they decided that heritage craft deserved a direct relationship with the people who carry it.

The studio opened with nine SKUs, a six-week waitlist, and no wholesale partners. By refusing wholesale entirely for the first three years, the brand built something rarer than distribution: loyalty. Today, 82% of customers purchase again. That number is not a metric — it is the architecture of everything that follows.

CRAFT
The Craft Method  ·  Five Stages
From raw hide
to heirloom — five stages,
no shortcuts.
01
Tannery Selection
We source exclusively from three vegetable-tanneries in Córdoba, Spain and Ponte a Egola, Italy — each certified by the Leather Working Group. Hides are inspected in-person by Cédric Villette every season.
Córdoba & Toscana
02
Hand-Splitting & Sorting
Each hide is split by hand to a precise 2.2 mm for primary surfaces, 1.4 mm for linings. Skins are graded into four categories; only Grade A and B enter production. Grade C is donated to apprenticeship schools.
2.2 mm — 1.4 mm
03
Saddle-Stitch Assembly
All exterior seams are hand-stitched using the saddle-stitch method — two needles, linen thread, 8 stitches per centimetre. No machines touch load-bearing seams. Assembly time per piece: 4–11 hours depending on complexity.
8 spi · linen thread
04
Edge-Finishing
Edges are burnished with beeswax and a heated bone folder through five successive passes. No painted edges are used. Each finished edge bears a micro-bevel that takes approximately 25 minutes to complete on a full-size tote.
5-pass beeswax burnish
05
QC & Provenance Card
Every finished piece undergoes a 48-point inspection protocol before shipment. A handwritten Provenance Card — naming the artisan, hide origin, and stitch count — ships with each order. Lifetime repair is offered at any atelier.
Lifetime repair promise

Our Arc  ·  2019–2027
The Transformation Arc
From a
Lyon workshop
to four continents.
Every decision Atelier No. 9 has made since 2019 has been an answer to the same question: what does it mean to make something that earns its place in someone's life for thirty years? The brand does not pursue scale for its own sake. It pursues depth — with customers, with craft partners, with the handful of retail spaces that carry the work with the care it deserves.
