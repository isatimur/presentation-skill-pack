Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 20, 2026 · Last updated: June 20, 2026

presentation-skill-pack is an open-source developer tool distributed as npm and PyPI packages under the MIT license. This policy describes what data is (and isn't) collected in connection with the website at presentation-skill-pack.vercel.app and the associated packages.

What we collect

Website analytics: The site is hosted on Vercel. Vercel may collect standard server-side access logs (IP address, user agent, referrer, timestamp) to operate the service. We do not use any first-party analytics, tracking pixels, or cookies beyond what Vercel requires to serve pages. Refer to Vercel's Privacy Policy for details.

npm and PyPI downloads: Download counts and aggregate statistics are collected by npm (npmjs.com) and the Python Package Index (pypi.org) when you install a package. We see aggregate download numbers; we do not receive personally identifiable data about individual installs.

GitHub: The source code repository is hosted at github.com/isatimur/presentation-skill-pack. Interactions such as stars, issues, and pull requests are governed by GitHub's Privacy Statement.

What we don't collect

The npm and PyPI packages run entirely on your local machine or inside your agent's environment. They do not phone home, send telemetry, or transmit any data about the decks you create, the content of your files, or your usage patterns.

Cookies

The website does not set first-party cookies. Vercel may set a small number of strictly-necessary technical cookies to serve the site.

Third-party services

The gallery pages load fonts from Google Fonts and icons from the Font Awesome CDN. These requests are made by your browser directly to those services and are subject to their respective privacy policies.

Data retention

We do not store personal data ourselves. Server logs held by Vercel are subject to Vercel's retention policies.

Your rights

Because we do not collect personal data directly, there is no personal data for us to provide, correct, or delete. If you believe Vercel, GitHub, npm, or PyPI holds personal data you'd like addressed, contact those services directly.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we will update the effective date above and, where appropriate, note the change in the GitHub repository.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised as a GitHub issue or by emailing swiirl.claude@gmail.com.
